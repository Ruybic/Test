Transparency & Player Rights

Everything you need to know about how Osu! Iraq Today operates, fetches data, and protects your privacy.

1. The OIT Manifesto

Osu! Iraq Today (OIT) is a community-driven project built to put Iraqi osu! talent on the map. We aren't just tracking numbers; we are curating a daily snapshot to celebrate the achievements, top plays, and creations of our local community.

We do not require users to sign in or create accounts because we only aggregate data that is already public. Think of us like a sports news website, but dedicated exclusively to the Top 500 Iraqi osu! players.

2. How the Magic Happens (Technical Data)

We believe in full transparency regarding how your data ends up on our site. We do not use web-scraping bots that strain servers or violate personal space.

The Daily Workflow

  • The Trigger: An automated GitHub workflow runs exactly once every 24 hours.
  • The Fetch: We request the top plays of the Top 500 Iraqi players directly through the official osu! API.
  • The Filter: We only pull submitted scores. Live plays or unsubmitted local scores are entirely invisible to us.
  • YouTube Integration: Community videos are embedded using the official YouTube API. This means every view on our site counts towards the original creator's channel. We do not re-upload or steal video files.

We do NOT track personal Twitter accounts, Discord messages, or IP addresses. Your personal space off the osu! client is respected 100%.

3. Player Rights & The "Untracked" Status

While everything we display is public data on the osu! network, we understand that some players prefer to grind in the shadows. Your comfort in the community is our priority.

How to Opt-Out

If you are in the Top 500 and do not wish to be featured on our leaderboards or daily recaps, you have the right to claim Untracked Status.

  • By requesting untracked status, your scores and profile will be filtered out of our daily workflows.
  • Your name on historical data will be replaced with [Hidden Player] to protect your identity.
  • No questions asked. No login required to prove who you are—just DM the developer on Discord.
